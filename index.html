ABOUT

The University of New Brunswick was late to the game in establishing a varsity sailing team. Dalhousie, Memorial and other universities in the region have had sailing teams for years, but the UNB Sailing Team was established only recently in the fall of 2017. The UNB sailing team is comprised of students with a wide range of experience levels and background. And the team has only experienced upwards growth since its inception.

The continuous growth of the team relies on interest from students at UNB. We are looking for enthusiastic students who are keen to learn and willing to invest their time to learn the ropes. We accept anyone and everyone, regardless of experience level, whether interested in leisure or competition sailing.

The UNB Sailing team is a provisional member of Canadian Intercollegiate Sailing Association (CISCA). The team is partnered with the Mactaquac Sailing Association and the Fredericton Yacht Club. The team is also partners with the Fredericton Small Craft And Aquatic Centre.

Brandon Richardson

Director of Operations

I'll admit, I don't have much experience sailing--especially when compared against others in the team. Prior to joining the UNB Sailing Team, I had roughly two years of casual keelboat sailing experience. The whole team has been incredibly receiving and encouraging despite this, which says a lot about the team and who can be a part of it. You don't need experience, you don't even need to participate in the competitive sailing! Just come have some fun, meet new people, and become part of a family.

Brandon is a fourth-year Software Engineering student. He is currently a member of the dingy racing team, and attends most keelboat races.

DONATE

We need help.

We receive limited funding from the university and community, and as a result it is difficult to arrange travel for students who wish to compete in intercollegiate sailing competitions. We also rely on membership fees to fund the rental of Dingy boats for training, which can be a significant cost for students. Donations and support from the community help keep the team sustainable and able to introduce students to the world of sailing.

All help is welcomed. We are looking to work with local businesses to form sponsorships through brand promotion. We are also seeking donations of any kind, such as monetary donations or the provision of sailing gear and equipment. If interested, please contact Todd Ward via email at toddpward@gmail.com.
